What You Need To Know

In this class, we will be creating the first of two final assignments necessary for a buyer user to gain access to a product. This assignment is called a 'Category Product Assignment', and creates the relationship between a product and a category. Remember, a product can never be assigned directly to a user. There must be a category in-between them to create the relationship. The next class will round out this relationship for the user.

Creating a Category Product Assignment

We will be focusing on the create.js script, but feel free to take a look at the others to see what kind of CRUD capabilities you have access to for Category Product Assignments.

Once you are in the create.js script, take a look at the object you will be creating stored in the assignment variable. As you can see, it is a pretty simple object. It has a CategoryID, ProductID, and ListOrder. Additionally, a Category Product Assignment requires a buyerID as a parameter, represented by the first line of code. Fill in the buyerID for whatever buyer your selected user is a part of. Then fill in the CategoryID and ProductID with those you have been saving for this class.

When you are ready, go ahead and hit 'Run'!
